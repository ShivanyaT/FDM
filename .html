Frequency-Division Multiplexing (FDM) is a technique where a wider bandwidth is divided into multiple narrower bands, each carrying a separate signal. This is often combined with Fourier analysis, particularly the Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT) and its efficient implementation, the Fast Fourier Transform (FFT), for digital implementation. In these digital FDM systems, the DFT is used for modulation and demodulation, allowing for the efficient transmission and reception of multiple signals simultaneously on different frequency bands.
